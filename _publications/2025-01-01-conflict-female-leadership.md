excerpt: 'Examines whether female leadership reduces violence during the Colombian conflict (late 1990s–2000s). Using close mayoral races between women and men as an identification strategy, I find that the election of female candidates correlates with a 60% decline in guerrilla attacks. The paper analyzes the spatial distribution and gender composition of guerrilla units, and uses text analysis of campaign statements to show that female mayors use more "peaceful" language while acknowledging conflict, consistent with a preference for de-escalation.'
